1.  Introduction
The National Digital Building System is a web-based property rental platform designed to digitize and streamline the process of listing, leasing, and managing residential and commercial properties. The system connects four key actor groups — Admins, Property Managers, Tenants, and Visitors — within a secure, regulated environment.
This document defines the functional and non-functional requirements, system actors and their responsibilities, data classes, and registration workflows for the platform.

3.  System Actors & Responsibilities
The system defines four distinct actor types. Each actor has a unique registration pathway and a specific set of permitted actions within the platform.
Actor
Registration Method
Core Responsibilities
Admin
Credentials hard-coded into the system; no self-registration.
Manage all users (Tenants, Property Managers, Visitors); track transactions per building; approve Property Manager registrations.
Property Manager
Self-registers; Admin reviews and approves after verifying property ownership proof and Telebirr merchant account.
Add, update, delete property listings; generate lease agreements; manage tenants and active leases; handle tenant complaints.
Tenant
Invited by property manager via QR code; completes sign-up, signs lease, and gains access only after landlord approval.
Search listings; rent a property; generate and sign lease agreements; pay via Telebirr; submit complaints.
Visitor
Self-registers with email and phone number; no approval required.
Browse and search property listings (read-only access).
4.  Registration Workflows
4.1  Admin
Admin credentials are pre-configured and hard-coded into the system at deployment. There is no self-registration flow for Admins.
4.2  Property Manager
Property Manager registration follows an admin-approval workflow:
The applicant fills in the registration form with personal and business details.
The applicant uploads proof of property ownership and their Telebirr merchant account credentials.
The Admin reviews the submission and either approves or rejects it.
Upon approval, the Property Manager account is activated and access is granted.
4.3  Visitor
Visitor registration is self-service and requires no approval:
The visitor provides their full name, email address, and phone number.
The account is created immediately with read-only access to property listings.
4.4  Tenant
Tenant registration is invitation-only and follows a multi-step workflow:
The Property Manager generates a unique QR code for a specific unit and shares it with the prospective tenant.: Step 1 – QR Invite
The tenant scans the QR code, which opens a form where they enter their email address and select the unit they wish to lease.: Step 2 – Tenant Initiates
The system sends a personalised invitation link to the tenant's email address.: Step 3 – Invitation Link
The tenant clicks the link and completes registration, providing their full name, kebele ID, phone number, and other required details.: Step 4 – Registration
The tenant reviews and digitally signs the generated lease agreement.: Step 5 – Lease Signing
The Property Manager reviews the signed agreement and approves the tenant, granting them full system access.: Step 6 – Landlord Approval
5.  Data Classes
The following table describes the primary data classes (entities) of the system and their key attributes.
Class
Key Attributes
Notes
Tenant
tenant_id, full_name, kebele_id, email, phone_number

manager_id, full_name, email, phone_number
Must hold a Telebirr merchant account; registration requires Admin approval.

admin_id, full_name, email

property_id, name_of_compound, owner_name, street_address, square_meters, lease_price, images

transaction_id, amount, timestamp
